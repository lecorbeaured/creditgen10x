SEC Eliminates PDT Rule 2026: Everything Traders Need to Know

For 25 years, the Pattern Day Trader rule kept millions of retail investors out of active day trading with a $25,000 minimum equity requirement. On April 14, 2026, that barrier was formally eliminated. Here's the complete breakdown.

What Happened

The U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission approved FINRA's proposed overhaul of Rule 4210 on April 14, 2026, in Release No. 34-105226. The approval replaces the Pattern Day Trader framework — including its $25,000 minimum equity requirement and four-trade-per-five-day limit — with a modern real-time intraday margin system.

The rule change was first proposed by FINRA in December 2025 under file number SR-FINRA-2025-017. A public comment period ran through February 2026, during which regulators received an overwhelming volume of feedback supporting the change.

What the Old Rule Was

Introduced in 2001 after the dot-com crash, the PDT rule designated any trader who made four or more day trades within a rolling five-business-day window in a margin account as a "Pattern Day Trader." That designation required maintaining at least $25,000 in account equity at all times. Dropping below that threshold meant a 90-day restriction on intraday trading — a quarter on the sidelines regardless of your skill or strategy.

What the New Framework Says

The new system under Rule 4210 eliminates both the PDT designation and the $25,000 minimum entirely. In their place: real-time intraday margin requirements based on actual position risk. Your broker now monitors the live exposure of every position you hold throughout the trading day. You must maintain enough equity to cover your real-time margin requirement — full stop. There are no trade limits.

The new minimum account equity to access intraday margin trading is $2,000 — though individual brokers may set higher minimums during the implementation period.

The 18-Month Rollout

FINRA must publish a Regulatory Notice within 45 days of the April 14 approval. The rule takes effect upon that publication. Brokers then have up to 18 months to fully upgrade their real-time margin calculation systems. Expect significant variation across platforms throughout 2026 and into 2027. Robinhood, Webull, and Cobra Trading are moving aggressively. Traditional institutional brokers are expected to move more slowly.

Market Reaction

Retail brokerage stocks immediately surged on the news. Robinhood rose over 7.6% and Webull climbed nearly 9% in early trading on April 15. Schwab and Fidelity, whose primary customer base skews toward long-term investors, saw minimal movement — reflecting the different exposure each platform has to active retail day traders.

What This Means for You

If you previously had less than $25,000 in your trading account, you can now day trade without restriction — provided your broker has implemented the new framework and your account maintains sufficient margin for your open positions. Frequently Asked Questions

Is this permanent or can the PDT rule come back?

The rule change amends FINRA Rule 4210 through the standard regulatory process. Reversing it would require another full rulemaking cycle — not impossible, but unlikely in the near term given the overwhelming public support for the change.

Do I need to do anything to have PDT restrictions removed?

No action is required from you. Once your broker implements the new framework, PDT restrictions will be lifted on your account automatically. Contact your broker to ask about their implementation timeline.

Does this apply to options trading?

Yes. The new intraday margin framework applies to equity and options positions. 0DTE options traders in particular will benefit from the elimination of trade-count restrictions.
