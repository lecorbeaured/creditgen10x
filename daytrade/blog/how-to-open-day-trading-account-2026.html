How to Open a Day Trading Account in 2026 (Post-PDT Guide)

Opening a day trading account used to mean scraping together $25,000. Now the minimum is $2,000 — but the decision of where and how to open your account matters more than ever during the broker rollout period.

Step 1: Choose Your Broker

During the 18-month rollout period, broker selection is more important than ever. Prioritize platforms that have explicitly announced PDT implementation: Robinhood, Webull, and Cobra Trading are ahead of the pack. Avoid platforms where you can't get a clear answer about their implementation timeline.

Step 2: Understand Margin vs. Cash Accounts

A cash account lets you trade without margin — no PDT issues, but trades take T+1 to settle, limiting your capital availability. A margin account gives you access to intraday leverage and the full flexibility of the new framework, but requires meeting margin requirements in real time. For active day trading, a margin account is what you want.

Step 3: Fund Your Account

The regulatory minimum is $2,000, but starting with more gives you better risk management headroom. A $5,000 account lets you risk 1% ($50) per trade — enough to make meaningful positions without overexposure. Fund via bank transfer (ACH) — typically 2-3 business days to settle and become available for trading.

Step 4: Ask the Right Questions Before Your First Trade

Before placing any intraday trade, confirm with your broker: Has PDT been removed from your account type? What is the current intraday margin requirement for equities? For options? Does the platform auto-liquidate on margin breach, or notify you first? What is the margin call process and timeline?

Step 5: Paper Trade First

Most major platforms offer paper trading (simulated trading with real market data). Use it for at minimum one full week before going live. Track every trade as if it were real money. Compare your paper results to your live results in week 2 — divergence reveals how emotion affects your execution.
