How the New Real-Time Intraday Margin System Works

Dynamic margin is the single most important concept for post-PDT traders to understand. Unlike the old fixed buying power system, your available capital now changes in real time throughout the trading day based on your actual position exposure.

What Is Real-Time Intraday Margin?

Under the old PDT framework, your broker assigned you a fixed amount of buying power at the start of each day. That number didn't change much unless you made a big losing trade. The new system is fundamentally different: your margin requirement is calculated continuously, position by position, based on the live risk exposure of everything you hold.

If a position moves against you, your required margin increases — immediately. If volatility spikes in the underlying security, your broker may require more capital to maintain the position — in real time. This is more sophisticated than trade counting, but it's also less forgiving.

How Intraday Margin Is Calculated

The exact formula varies by broker, but the core inputs are consistent: the current market price of your position, the implied or realized volatility of the security, your net directional exposure across all open positions, and your available account equity as a buffer. High-volatility stocks require more margin per dollar of exposure than stable large-caps.

What This Means Practically

You can open a trade at 9:45 AM with sufficient margin and find yourself under-margined by 10:15 AM if the position moves 8% against you. This is not a notification situation — brokers can and will auto-liquidate positions in real time to bring your account back into compliance. Understanding this dynamic is not optional. It is the core mechanic of the new system.
