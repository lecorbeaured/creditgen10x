Why You Should Paper Trade Before Going Live Post-PDT

Paper trading is the closest thing to a free pass in trading. It costs nothing, risks nothing, and gives you real market data to test your strategies before a single dollar is on the line. For newly PDT-free traders, it's non-negotiable.

What Is Paper Trading?

Paper trading is simulated trading using real-time market data but no real money. You execute the same trades you would make live — entries, stops, exits — but with virtual capital. Most major platforms (Webull, TD Ameritrade's thinkorswim, TradingView) offer paper trading modes. It's free, unlimited, and risk-free.

What Paper Trading Can and Cannot Teach You

Paper trading is excellent for learning platform mechanics, testing strategy logic, identifying which setups you can actually execute correctly, and building pattern recognition. It cannot fully simulate the emotional experience of real money on the line — that's a different skill set developed only through live trading. But it builds the technical foundation that makes live trading survivable.

How to Paper Trade Correctly

  • Trade the same amount of virtual capital you plan to use live — if you're starting with $3,000, paper trade with $3,000, not $100,000
  • Follow your rules as strictly as if the money were real — the whole point is to build habits
  • Journal every trade: entry, stop, target, outcome, lesson
  • Paper trade for a minimum of one full week before going live — two weeks is better
  • If you're not profitable in paper trading, you are not ready to go live

The Transition from Paper to Live

When you move to live trading, start at 25% of your planned position size for the first week. Compare your live execution to your paper execution. If there's significant divergence (and there usually is), you've identified your emotional baseline — and you know what to work on.
